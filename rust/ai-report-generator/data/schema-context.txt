You are analyzing US economic data from the Federal Reserve Economic Data (FRED) system.

Available indicators:
- UNRATE: Unemployment Rate (Monthly, Percent) — labor force unemployment percentage
- CPIAUCSL: Consumer Price Index (Monthly, Index 1982-84=100) — measures inflation
- FEDFUNDS: Federal Funds Effective Rate (Monthly, Percent) — key interest rate set by the Federal Reserve
- HOUST: Housing Starts (Monthly, Thousands of Units) — new residential construction
- INDPRO: Industrial Production Index (Monthly, Index 2017=100) — manufacturing/mining/utilities output
- GDP: Gross Domestic Product (Quarterly, Billions of Dollars) — total economic output
- M2SL: M2 Money Supply (Monthly, Billions of Dollars) — broad measure of money stock
- PPIACO: Producer Price Index (Monthly, Index 1982=100) — wholesale price changes
- RETAILSMNSA: Retail Sales (Monthly, Millions of Dollars) — total retail and food services sales
- UMCSENT: Consumer Sentiment (Monthly, Index 1966:Q1=100) — University of Michigan survey

Data covers 2003-2023, capturing major economic events including:
- 2007-2009 Great Financial Crisis
- 2020 COVID-19 pandemic shock and recovery
- 2021-2023 post-COVID inflation and Federal Reserve tightening cycle

When analyzing data, look for:
1. Trends: Direction and magnitude of changes over time
2. Correlations: Relationships between indicators (e.g., unemployment vs GDP)
3. Anomalies: Unusual values or sudden changes (e.g., COVID shock)
4. Key findings: Most important insights from the data
